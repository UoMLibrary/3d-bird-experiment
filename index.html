Eskimo Curlew

Numenius borealis

About

The Eskimo Curlew was once one of the most numerous shorebirds in North America. Market hunting and the loss of its prairie stopover habitat collapsed the population in the late nineteenth century. The last confirmed sighting was in 1963, and the species is now presumed extinct.

This is a photogrammetry scan of a mounted specimen in the collection of Manchester Museum, decimated from 3.6 million to roughly 178,000 triangles for the web, with a 4K colour texture.
